179 BC

Area incorporated into China as part of the Han Dynasty.

939

Vietnam achieved independence from China after centuries of rule.

1406 - 1407

Vietnamese independence was threatened by the Chinese Ming Dynasty, leading to resistance and defense efforts.

1859 - 1885

France began conquering Vietnam. Resistance movements emerged, including opposition to Christianization.

1887

French colonial leadership imposed major cultural and political changes on Vietnamese society.

1930

The Yen Bai Mutiny led by the Vietnamese Nationalist Party was crushed by the French, pushing some members toward communist ideas.

1940

During World War II, Japan invaded and occupied Vietnam.

1941

The Viet Minh, led by Ho Chi Minh, organized resistance against the Japanese occupation.

1946

After Japan's defeat, the Viet Minh began war against the French to gain independence.

1954

Vietnam became divided after agreements involving the French and Bao Dai government established separate northern and southern states.

1960

North Vietnam aligned with communist nations like the Soviet Union, while South Vietnam aligned with the United States and democratic allies.

1965

United States military involvement sharply increased, becoming central to the war effort in South Vietnam.

1970 - 1973

The United States gradually withdrew troops from Vietnam following mounting casualties and public pressure.

1975

South Vietnam fell to North Vietnamese forces, leading to the reunification of Vietnam under communist rule.

1978

Vietnam invaded Cambodia after attacks on Vietnamese villages and overthrew the Cambodian government.
