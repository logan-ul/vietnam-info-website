Welcome to Vietnam!

Vietnam is a country in the Southeast Asia region. It's famous for its beautiful geography, food, and friendly people. The country has a long history, with several instances of fighting for their freedom. Vietnam was ruled by China, and later by France, but the people always stayed true to their identity. Many countries tried to take control of Vietnam, but the people never gave up.

Vietnam is known for its hardy culture and traditions. People celebrate with many festivals and enjoying family time. The food is tasty, and unique, like pho and banh mi. The country has many interesting places to visit, such as old cities, temples, and beautiful nature. It is a place where the past and present come together, making it special and welcoming to everyone.

Government

Vietnam has a nationally elected assembly, a president, a prime minister, and a cabinet. The prime minister and the president also have deputy ministers and a vice president respectively. The prime minister handles different ministries that are similar to departments. The current president is To Lam, and the current prime minister is Le Minh Hung. The leading party has been the Vietnamese Communist Party since they won the war in the 70’s. All power is held by the government which is ruled by the Vietnamese Communist Party. They use a communist based system with socialist ideas.

Culture

Most of Vietnam is centered around Confucianism. They respect their family and elders over everything. Family is patrilineal in structure, meaning that men carry the lineage. However, Woman still play major roles in work, finances, and raising children. While most Vietnamese people practice the idea of Confucianism, they also practice ideas and rituals much like the Mexican “Day of the Dead”. The similarities being their worship of the dead along with the way they honor and respect them. A major holiday is Tet Nguyen Dan, or more commonly known as Tet, which is a Lunar New Year celebration. You can think of it like any holiday we have here, people clean their houses, visit each other, and eat good food. A special quirk, however, is that Vietnamese people do is handing out red envelopes full of money for good luck. Food takes a big role in Vietnamese Culture as in most. Fish products are a popular inclusion, Vietnam being a mostly costal country. Overall Culture is centered around hard work, food, and family.

Society Today

Society in Vietnam today is a mix of tradition and modernization. Respect for elders and family still persists and community ties are still highly valued. While these traditional beliefs are highly valued, cities are becoming more advanced over time. Woman can take roles in almost any position today, even presidency, however they are still expected to hold some traditional expectations as well. Education is extremely valued. Literacy rates are around 95% or higher, meaning most regular adults can read and write. Unemployment is really good at around 1.5%

Religion

Vietnam has a mainly unreligious population. They do, however, have a sort of folk religion where they do certain ceremonies and practices. There are also minorities of Catholicism, Buddhism, and Hoahoaism, but the vast majority don’t have a deity or God. Ethnicities wise they mainly have Viet, an Asian race unique to Vietnam. The French spent a long time trying to invade and colonize Vietnam, and as such they mixed the two cultures a bit. Catholicism was never a religion within Vietnam before the French conflict. There are other minorities within Vietnam but they’re not large enough to take count of.
